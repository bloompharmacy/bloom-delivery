Part One

The Problem

Before the platform, every delivery depended on memory, paper and phone calls — and nobody could answer a simple question with confidence: "Where is that order right now?"

Daily Reality

What Every Branch Was Living With

These weren't occasional issues — they were the normal working day.

Orders Went Missing
A package handed to a driver with no record. No proof of who took it, when, or where it went.
"Where Is My Order?"
Customer calls the branch. The branch calls the driver. The driver is mid-route. Everyone waits.
No Real Numbers Until Later
Daily totals were assembled by hand at the end of the day — too late to actually manage anything.
Money Disputes
Delivery fees, driver payouts and returned orders argued from memory, with no shared record to settle it.
The Same Order, Twice
Nothing stopped one order being handed out two times — wasted trips and double-counted revenue.
No View Across Branches
Each branch was an island. Comparing performance meant collecting numbers by phone.
Part Two

The Solution

Three simple apps — one for each person in the chain — all writing to one shared, live record of every order.

The Platform

Three Apps. One Shared Truth.

Each person sees only what they need — and every action updates the same live record instantly.

The Handover App
Branch Staff
Staff scan each package at the counter, choose the delivery zone, and assign it to a driver. The order is registered the moment it leaves the branch — with a name, a time and a price attached.
The Driver App
Drivers · Arabic
Drivers open their phone and see the orders assigned to them appear automatically. They confirm the outcome — delivered, partially delivered or returned — and record the payment taken.
The Manager Dashboard
Managers
Managers see live numbers across every branch — revenue, delivery times, driver performance — approve returns, settle transfers, and export finance-ready reports.
All three connect to one live database
When the driver taps "Delivered," the manager's dashboard reflects it — no syncing, no exporting, no waiting.
How It Works

The Life of a Single Order

From counter to customer — every step recorded automatically.

1
Scanned at the counter
Staff scan the package, pick the delivery zone, and assign a driver. The system blocks the same order being sent out twice.
2
Appears on the driver's phone
No phone call needed — the order shows up in the driver's list within moments of being handed over.
3
Driver confirms the outcome
Delivered, partially delivered, or returned — plus the amount collected and how it was paid.
4
Time and performance calculated
The system measures how long the delivery took and compares it to the target time set for that zone.
5
Manager sees it — and the money is counted correctly
Returned orders wait for manager approval before their fee counts toward revenue. Nothing is credited by accident.

Part Four

Our Evolution

We didn't buy this system. We built it, outgrew our first version, and rebuilt it properly.

The Journey

From Spreadsheet to Real Platform

Stage One — Google Sheets
Our first attempt at getting orders out of notebooks and into something shared. It proved the idea was right.
Stage Two — Sheets + Apps Script
We added automation on top of the spreadsheet. It worked — until the volume grew.
Stage Three — The Firebase Platform
A purpose-built system on Google's cloud database: three real apps, a real database, and automated servers doing the counting.
Why we had to move on
It got slow. The more orders we added, the longer everything took to open and update.
It was clunky on phones. Drivers were working from a spreadsheet interface never designed for a phone in one hand.
Nothing was truly live. People worked from their own copy of the truth, and the copies disagreed.
One wrong click was permanent. A deleted row was simply gone — no safety net.
It couldn't grow with us. More branches and more drivers meant more strain, not more capability.
The Tech Leap

What Changed When We Rebuilt

Four upgrades that turned a spreadsheet workaround into a business system.

A Real Database
Instead of rows in a file, every order is a proper record with rules protecting it. Two people can work at once without overwriting each other.
Real-Time Syncing
Changes appear on every screen at once. The driver's confirmation reaches the manager's dashboard immediately — no refresh, no export.
Automated Servers Doing the Counting
Daily totals are calculated by a background service the moment an order changes — not by a person, and not by the phone in someone's pocket. The numbers are always current and always consistent.
Automatic Backups & Recovery
The database is backed up every day automatically, and we can rewind it to any moment in the past seven days. A mistake is now recoverable instead of permanent.
